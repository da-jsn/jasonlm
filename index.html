The 40-Minute Grant Workflow

A practical grant-writing workflow built around funder research, source packs, tailored project instructions, and focused drafting.

  • Research the funder deeply
  • Build a source pack
  • Draft one question at a time
  • Verify claims, numbers, and fit
  • Keep final judgment human

One capacity leak. One small safe experiment.

From Chaos to Capacity

A workshop and resource-pack approach for turning one advocacy bottleneck into one responsible AI or automation test.

  • Pick a repeated, painful workflow
  • Map where capacity leaks
  • Choose AI, automation, or a clearer human process
  • Add privacy and values checks
  • Test for one week

The review step is part of the system.

Meeting-to-Actions

A simple way to turn notes into clear decisions, action items, owners, deadlines, open questions, and follow-ups.

  • Summarize decisions
  • Extract action items
  • Flag missing owners or deadlines
  • Create follow-up reminders
  • Keep sensitive content out or anonymized

AI-aware does not mean AI-always.

AI & Automation Onboarding

Staff onboarding that helps teams become more confident and practical with AI, without hype, shame, or unsafe data use.

  • Use real work examples
  • Set safe-use guardrails
  • Practice prompting basics
  • Spot automation candidates
  • Build human review habits

Principles and guardrails

Useful, trustworthy, and human-led

Start with workflow pain

AI is useful when it solves a real bottleneck.

Keep humans in charge

Strategy, values, relationships, approvals, and accountability stay human.

Protect privacy

Minimize data, redact sensitive details, and use approved tools.

Verify facts

Especially for policy, legal, scientific, financial, funder-facing, and public claims.

Build small and reusable

Turn what works into a prompt, SOP, template, or automation.

Avoid hype

The aim is to save time, reduce stress, improve quality, and build capacity for animals.

We are not trying to automate our values. We are trying to protect capacity for the work only humans can do.
