I'm not coming from an IT kind of background. I'm coming from nonprofit leadership, animal advocacy, and years of testing AI on work that actually needed to get done.

Grounded in the work

Not from IT. From the work.

I've been using AI since before ChatGPT made it normal to talk about. Back then, it was clunky and weird and still somehow useful. As the tools got better, I got better at turning them into actual workflows.

That matters because this is not abstract AI advice. It comes from day-to-day nonprofit leadership: grant deadlines, campaign planning, volunteer coordination, reporting, comms, and all the tiny operational things that quietly eat a team alive.

At Dyrenes Alliance, AI, automation, and better workflows now save me about 1-1.5 days a week. That is a massive difference. It means more time for the work that actually moves things forward.

That is why I want to share this. If other organizations in the movement can get even a slice of that capacity back, they can do more good and help save as many animals as possible.

The starting point is always the work. What keeps coming back? Where does the team lose time? What needs a human decision, and what could be made easier with a prompt, a template, a source pack, an SOP, or a small automation?

Start with the task that keeps coming back. Make it easier once, then make that easier to repeat.

The 40-Minute Grant Workflow

A grant-writing workflow for teams that want AI to help with the heavy lift, without pretending the grant writes itself.

  • Research the funder deeply
  • Build a source pack
  • Draft one question at a time
  • Verify claims, numbers, and fit
  • Keep final judgment human

One capacity leak. One small safe experiment.

From Chaos to Capacity

A workshop and resource-pack approach for turning one repeated bottleneck into one AI-first workflow your team can try quickly.

  • Pick a repeated, painful workflow
  • Map where capacity leaks
  • Choose AI, automation, or a clearer human process
  • Add privacy and values checks
  • Test for one week

The review step is part of the system.

Meeting-to-Actions

A simple way to turn messy notes into clear decisions, action items, owners, deadlines, open questions, and follow-ups.

  • Summarize decisions
  • Extract action items
  • Flag missing owners or deadlines
  • Create follow-up reminders
  • Keep sensitive content out or anonymized

AI-first does not mean AI-only.

AI & Automation Onboarding

  • Build human review habits

Principles and guardrails

Useful, trustworthy, and human-led

Use AI on real work

Not abstract demos. Actual tasks your team already has.

Keep humans in charge

Strategy, values, relationships, approvals, and accountability stay human.

Protect privacy

Minimize data, redact sensitive details, and use approved tools.

Verify facts

Especially for policy, legal, scientific, financial, funder-facing, and public claims.

Build small and reusable

Turn what works into a prompt, SOP, template, or automation.
